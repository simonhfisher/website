Making your own cocktail ingredients

Every item you could ever possibly want for a cocktail can be bought from somewhere, however ingredients could be marked
at a price thats cost prohibitive or simply not good value for money, even if thats not the case finding certain things might be
challenging in your local area and waiting for delivery might be inconvenient. Even if thats not the case maybe you can only
buy larger amounts than what you need for the few drinks. With that in mind I like to make most of my own syrups and
infusions because I can make them to my own specs and make as little or much as I like, they also work out way cheaper
and If I do make too much I can always freeze them for use another time. Here's a simple recipe that is quick and easy to
make.

    Sugar Syrup

    Ingredients

  • 200g of caster sugar - you can also use muscovado or demerara sugar,
    they have their own unique flavours and are great for Daiquiris
  • 200g of water - measured by weight for exact match in ingredients

    Method

  • Add your ingredients to a pan and put on low to medium heat
  • Stir intermittently to help dissolve
  • When liquid is clear and bubbles are coming are starting to come to
    surface turn off and allow to cool
  • Bottle and store in fridge
  • Optionally add a small amount of a spirit to prolong shelf life
