The Daiquiri

Because of my limited time on this project, I have cut my scope down to a single drink rather than a multitude, The drink I have
chosen is the Daiquiri, its origins are not entirely clear but it was possibly created by an American mining engineer called
Jennings Stockton Cox. The story goes that Cox was hosting hosting a party and had run out gin, not wanting to serve his
American guests dry rum, he made the cocktail with the ingredients he had. It was given the name Daiquiri after its birthplace
the iron mines of Daiquiri in Cuba.

    Ingredients

  • 50ml white rum - Havana Club 3 year old for that authentic Cuban taste
  • 25ml lime juice - fresh lime over bottled for more bite
  • 15ml or 20ml or 25ml sugar syrup - accounting for personal taste
  • 3 to 5 cubes of ice

    Method

  • Add your ingredients to your cocktail shaker
  • Shake vigorously until well chilled - about 20 to 30 seconds
  • Strain into a chilled coup glass or alternatively a short
    tumbler with a fresh cube of ice
  • Optionally garnish with a wheel of lime
